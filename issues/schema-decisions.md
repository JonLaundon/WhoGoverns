  - **~~OPEN — `derived_from_record_id` used two ways~~ — CORRECTED 2026-07-20 on inspection. This was NOT an inconsistency and the original note was wrong.** All 8 vetoes share a `provision_key` with the power they derive from, so A2.5 (one provision, one canonical record) holds throughout. The 4–4 split is by *holder identity*, and it tracks two genuinely different **statutory shapes**: **(a) embedded consent** — "A may, *with the consent of B*, do X": one provision confers A's power and embeds B's gate, so the canonical record is A's power and `derived_from` points at the **blocked** actor (Treasury s.153; SoS s.24(1)(b), s.6(1)(b), s.7(2)(b)); **(b) free-standing block** — "B may direct A not to…": the provision confers B's *own* blocking power while A's power sits elsewhere, so the canonical record is B's power and `derived_from` points at the **holder** (CMA s.16A; EA s.38; NE s.28E, s.28H). The model is behaving correctly in both. **The real gap is different and narrower:** in shape (b) the *gated* power is recorded nowhere. `veto-cma-wia1991-s16a-veto` blocks Ofwat's s.13 modification power and we hold `power-ofwat-wia1991-s13`, but nothing links them — so the delivery chain traverses at body granularity, not power granularity. Compounding: the EA/NE records are shape (b) *and* block private parties, so they have neither a blocked body nor a gated power, which is why they are the three edge-less vetoes. See the `blocks_power_id` proposal below.

- **2026-07-20 — VETO STRENGTH AUDIT: 5 of 8 vetoes re-graded; vocabularies given definitions.** Prompted by the sponsor noticing that **all 8 vetoes carried `strength: hard_stop`** — the signature of a missing rubric rather than eight independent judgements. **Root cause: every one of the 17 vocab files was a bare list of terms with no definitions**, so the extractor had no criteria to distinguish `hard_stop` from `strong_delay`. Second contributing cause: `calibration/confidence_log.csv` had **never received a powers-layer record** (30 rows, all from the 2026-07-06 body-classification pass), so A9.3's calibration mechanism could not surface the error. Audited in three tiers:
  - **Tier 1 — internal coherence (free, no source needed).** Three records asserted `hard_stop` while also recording `overridable: yes` *and naming the override*; two asserted `hard_stop` on `overridable: unknown`. Now enforced in `validate.py` (`veto_strength_errors()`) as a hard **error**: hard_stop+overridable:yes is a contradiction, hard_stop+unknown is unsupported, strong_delay requires overridable:yes, and any override must be cited. 5 new tests in `tests/test_veto_strength_audit.py`.
  - **Tier 2 — override sweep** (each veto re-read against its instrument **and adjacent sections**, since appeal routes rarely sit in the cited provision). Findings: **WRA 1991 s.38** → `strong_delay` (appeal to SoS under **s.43**; the previously-recorded override, a drought order, was the *wrong mechanism* — a separate power on separate grounds). **WCA 1981 s.28E** → `strong_delay` (appeal under **s.28F(5)**, SoS may "direct Natural England to give consent", plus a 4-month deemed-refusal clock). **WCA 1981 s.28H** → `procedural_risk` (under s.28H(4)–(6) a public body may proceed 28 days after notice regardless of assent). **WIA 1991 ss.6(1)(b), 7(2)(b)** → strength **confirmed** `hard_stop`, but `overridable` corrected yes→no: a "general authorisation" is given *by the Secretary of State*, i.e. the same holder consenting in class, not a route around the consent. **WIA 1991 ss.16A, 153, 24(1)(b)** → `hard_stop` confirmed against primary text.
  - **Tier 3 — schema-blind eval.** An independent agent re-graded all 8 from primary sources **without being shown the existing labels**, and **agreed 8/8** with the post-audit gradings — independently deriving the same-holder rule and independently flagging s.28H as not a veto. Convergent rather than self-confirming, so all 8 rise to `verification_status: machine_verified` (A10). High-impact vetoes still need a human double-check before publication.
  - **Vocabularies given definitions (the root fix).** `veto_strength` v0.2, `veto_type` v0.3, `modality` v0.2, `legal_effect` v0.2, `power_type` v0.2, `duty_type` v0.2 — each term now carries a `definition` and an operational `test`, plus per-vocab rules: the **override sweep**, the **same-holder rule**, the **wrong-mechanism trap**, and `veto_type`'s **gateway test** ("can the other actor lawfully proceed if the holder says no?"). **Provenance stated honestly: the four strength terms are PROJECT VOCABULARY — no government document defines "hard_stop".** They are anchored to the mandatory/directory doctrine in **The Judge Over Your Shoulder, 6th ed (GLD, 2022), paras 2.57–2.60** (new source record `source-guidance-judge-over-your-shoulder-2022`, methodological use only — never cited as authority for a power/duty/veto record). Checked and rejected: the **OPC drafting guidance does not cover consent/concurrence/consultation** (its only relevant line is that office policy avoids the legislative "shall"). - **2026-07-06 — DECISION (sponsor, Jon): officials/offices are first-class, power-holding nodes — NOT a body_type.** Rationale: most UK statutory powers vest in an **office** ("the Secretary of State may…", "the Prime Minister shall…", "the Treasury may…"), which in law is a **corporation sole** — a continuous legal person distinct from both the human holder and the hosting department. Modelling consequences:
  - **Do not** add PM/ministers to `body_type` (that would collapse people into machines). They are the **Office / PersonRole** layer (already first-class in A4; leave draft in Spiral 1 per the A4.206 exception).
  - **Annex A6 revision needed (flag for sponsor):** the draft Power/Duty/Veto records bind to `body_id` only. They must gain a **polymorphic `holder`** — `office_id` OR `body_id` — so office-held powers are expressible without mis-attributing them to a department. (The annex's own Duty example already reads `"trigger": "When the Secretary of State proposes to…"`, i.e. the actor is the office.) Build in **Spiral 2** when Power/Duty/Veto leave draft; recorded now so officials cannot be quietly skipped.
  - **Spiral 1 presentation:** the map is **office-centred** — PM's office → Cabinet offices → other ministerial offices → departments → agencies/ALBs — with Office as a node, not a department attribute. See the Spiral 1 workpack note (tasks 4 and 6).

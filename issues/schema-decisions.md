# Schema decisions log

- **2026-07-06 — Spiral 1.5 bolt-on: FUNCTIONAL axis added (`functions[]` + `function_source_ids[]` on Body).** A second, multi-valued classification axis orthogonal to `body_type`, so cross-cutting groupings (chiefly "regulator") are queryable/filterable without reopening decision #12. Rationale confirmed by the data: the well-known regulators span four body_types (Ofgem/ORR/CMA `non_ministerial_department`, Ofcom/FCA `other_body`, CQC/ICO `executive_ndpb`, ONR `public_corporation`) — regulation cannot be a body_type. Vocab `vocab/functions.json` v0.1 (10 terms: regulation, inspection, advisory, funding, research, cultural_heritage, ombudsman_redress, tribunal_justice, operational_delivery, standards_accreditation). Populated this pass: **`regulation` only**, from the DBT "List of UK regulators" (`source-official-dataset-list-of-uk-regulators`, OGL v3.0), exact normalised name/alias match (no fuzzy), 75 bodies tagged, each cited via `function_source_ids`. Other terms defined-but-unpopulated; fill from official sources later (in Spiral 2 the tag becomes derivable from statutory powers). Pipeline step `pipeline/refine_functions.py` (stdlib ODS reader, no new dependency). Map: gold "Highlight regulators" toggle + a "Functions" legend index (`functions` carried onto graph nodes by compile.py). **Decision register (plan §10): #12 stands (regulation is not a body_type); the function axis is the additive mechanism — worth a one-line note on #12 when the plan is next touched.** Also: WSRA gained the sourced alias "Ofwat" (the DBT list pairs the two names) and its display name lost a trailing space.

- **2026-07-06 — Office schema: `office_type` re-based on the governance tiers (decision #13).** Enum changed from rank labels (secretary_of_state/minister_of_state/parliamentary_under_secretary/permanent_secretary/other) to `prime_minister | cabinet_minister | junior_minister | civil_servant | independent_official | other`. `civil_servant`/`independent_official` are reserved for the deferred senior-officials layer (not populated in Spiral 1). Task 4 assigns from the role title via a documented heuristic (Secretary of State + named cabinet posts → cabinet_minister; Minister of State / Parliamentary Under-Secretary / whips / junior Treasury secretaries / Solicitor General → junior_minister; exact "Prime Minister" → prime_minister; else → other, flagged in notes). 15 of 125 offices are `other` (ceremonial/special posts, envoys, the PM's secondary titles) — the honest review tail.
- **2026-07-06 — Refine R1: classification from Cabinet Office Public Bodies Directory (rank-2 source).** The GOV.UK `format` (rank 1) is coarse; the CO Public Bodies Directory 2023/24 (rank 2, A3.2) is authoritative for ALB classification. `refine_classification.py`: (A) exact-name match → adopt CO classification, add CO SourceRecord to `classification_source_ids`, clear the review flag (208 confirmed, 17 reclassified); (B) still-flagged bodies whose official name contains an advisory phrase → advisory_ndpb (10). **Fuzzy matching deliberately rejected** — it mis-mapped Arts Council of Wales→England, Royal Mint→Royal Mint Advisory Committee, two different Lottery funds. Net: advisory_ndpb 59→77, executive_agency 43→45, other_body 208→186, executive_ndpb 132→137; flags 338→314. New dependency: openpyxl (reads the source xlsx). New source: `source-official-dataset-cabinet-office-public-bodies`. CO does not cover non-ALB bodies (Bank of England, national parks, devolved, courts, sub-orgs) — those stay flagged, correctly.

- **2026-07-06 — DECISION (sponsor, Jon): body_type taxonomy re-based on the machineryofgovernment.uk category scheme (pattern only, no data reuse).** `body_type` vocab → v0.2; body schema enum updated; format map → v0.2. Changes vs v0.1:
  - **Dropped `regulator`.** Regulation is a *function* (captured via powers, Spiral 2), not a structural type. Regulators keep their statutory form — Ofgem was already seeded `non_ministerial_department`, so no seed churn.
  - **Renamed** `non_departmental_public_body` → `executive_ndpb`; `advisory_body` → `advisory_ndpb`; `other` → `other_body`. Reseeded the one affected record (ACOBA → `advisory_ndpb`).
  - **Added `division_directorate`** — target for the API `"Sub organisation"` format (126 live). Heterogeneous; review-flagged.
  - **Added `royal_charter_body`**, with a hard caveat: chartered bodies (Law Society of Scotland, ICAEW, RCVS, CWGC, Royal Hospital Chelsea, etc.) are **absent from the GOV.UK Organisations API** (verified: 0 of 8 sampled present). They are self-governing bodies incorporated via the Privy Council, deliberately outside the government org register. So `royal_charter_body` is **NOT populated from the API** — it is a separate primary-sourced tranche from the Privy Council chartered-bodies register, each record cited to its charter (A2.2), sequenced like the budgets/staffing bolt-on. No Spiral 1 API body auto-classifies to it. Open scope question for that tranche: several chartered bodies are professional self-regulators, not government bodies — decide inclusion when building it.
  - **Courts** (24 live) → `other_body` + review flag (constitutionally distinct from tribunals; possible dedicated `court` class later).
  - Rule retained (AGENT.md rule 3): any unmapped `format` → `other_body` + review flag, never dropped. Coarse/coerced formats also carry a review marker (see `flag_for_review` in the map).
  - **Decision register (plan §10):** logged as new decision **#12** (Closed 2026-07-06). (Note: register #10 is the *Tranche A0 body list*, a different item — not this taxonomy; an earlier draft of this note misread it.)
- **2026-07-06 — DECISION (sponsor, Jon): officials/offices are first-class, power-holding nodes — NOT a body_type.** Rationale: most UK statutory powers vest in an **office** ("the Secretary of State may…", "the Prime Minister shall…", "the Treasury may…"), which in law is a **corporation sole** — a continuous legal person distinct from both the human holder and the hosting department. Modelling consequences:
  - **Do not** add PM/ministers to `body_type` (that would collapse people into machines). They are the **Office / PersonRole** layer (already first-class in A4; leave draft in Spiral 1 per the A4.206 exception).
  - **Annex A6 revision needed (flag for sponsor):** the draft Power/Duty/Veto records bind to `body_id` only. They must gain a **polymorphic `holder`** — `office_id` OR `body_id` — so office-held powers are expressible without mis-attributing them to a department. (The annex's own Duty example already reads `"trigger": "When the Secretary of State proposes to…"`, i.e. the actor is the office.) Build in **Spiral 2** when Power/Duty/Veto leave draft; recorded now so officials cannot be quietly skipped.
  - **Spiral 1 presentation:** the map is **office-centred** — PM's office → Cabinet offices → other ministerial offices → departments → agencies/ALBs — with Office as a node, not a department attribute. See the Spiral 1 workpack note (tasks 4 and 6).

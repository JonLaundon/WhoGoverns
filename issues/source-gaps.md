# Source gaps

- **Ministers -> department mapping.** Not carried by the GOV.UK Organisations API. Confirm a machine-readable route (content API / ministers index at gov.uk/government/ministers) at the start of the build session; fallback is a small hand-built ~25-row table, each row cited. Blocks the Office/PersonRole records only.
- **Department budget (OSCAR) and staffing (Civil Service Statistics) reachability.** To test from the sandbox at build start; if bulk files are not reachable, becomes a one-off manual download by the sponsor.
- **Classification-refinement pass — NEXT after the structural graph (decision A, 2026-07-06).** Task 2 classifies from the GOV.UK Organisations API `format` field alone (source-hierarchy rank 1 for identity, but *coarse* for governance class). 338 of 663 bodies carry `needs_classification_review=true` — the `other_body` catch-all (208) plus the `division_directorate`/`Sub organisation` tail (130). To get the governance-based split (like machineryofgovernment.uk, but rebuilt from our own sources — MoG is comparison/gap-finding only per A2.1, never copied), a follow-up pass should bring in the **Cabinet Office Public Bodies dataset (rank 2, official)**, which classifies ALBs (executive/advisory NDPB, tribunal, etc.) directly. Expected effect: shrink the flagged pile, and reconcile toward MoG's aggregation (e.g. the API lists 29 tribunal *chambers* where MoG aggregates to ~7 — a granularity choice we make with our own audit trail). Workpack task 7 (side-by-side vs MoG counts) feeds this.
- **`jurisdiction` not in the Organisations API.** Every task-2 Body record defaults `jurisdiction: ["UK"]` because the API carries no jurisdiction field. Per-body geographic scope (England-only, Scotland-only, etc.) is a later refinement — from body names, framework docs or legislation — not sourced in Spiral 1.
- **In-formation bodies included (11).** `govuk_status` joining/transitioning → Body `status: forming` (raw value in notes; rendered dashed). These are bodies mid-formation (e.g. Local Audit Office, National Shipbuilding Office); revisit their classification when they go live.
- **Relationships (task 3) — edges to closed bodies dropped (180).** The sponsor/parent graph keeps only edges where both endpoints are held (live/forming) Body records. 180 parent/child links point at `closed` orgs (Crown Commercial Service, Infrastructure and Projects Authority, Government Equalities Office, etc. — all superseded by MoG changes) and are dropped as historical links (workpack §2). If a full historical graph is later wanted, these can be recovered from the raw cache.
- **Jointly-sponsored bodies (14).** 7 bodies have >1 department parent (e.g. Office for Zero Emission Vehicles → DESNZ + DfT; Public Sector Fraud Authority → Cabinet Office + HMT) and 7 have >1 non-department parent. **All** parent edges are kept as Relationship records; the Body's single `sponsor_department_id`/`parent_body_id` convenience field takes the first alphabetically (deterministic) — the entity page should read the Relationship records to show every sponsor, not just the body field.
- **6 live bodies whose only parent is closed** may show as roots (no live sponsor edge). Minor; revisit in the refinement pass.
- **Old-name aliases carried onto live successors (enrich_aliases.py, 2026-07-06).** 592 GOV.UK `closed` orgs are NOT all defunct: 201 are renames/merges/replacements whose live successor we already hold. The enrichment pass copies the old name (+ abbreviation) onto the successor's `other_names` for `changed_name`/`replaced` closures with a single held successor — 87 successors, 160 aliases (e.g. "Atomic Weapons Establishment"/"AWE" → AWE Nuclear Security Technologies; DIT → Department for Business and Trade). Conservative on purpose: `merged` (a distinct body absorbed into another, e.g. National School of Government → Cabinet Office), `devolved`, `split` and `no_longer_exists` are excluded — those are succession history, not alternate names.
- **Ministers (task 4): joint / cross-department roles (23).** 23 offices appear under more than one organisation in the content API (e.g. Minister for Women and Equalities across DfE + DWP; the PM's roles across PMO + Cabinet Office; a Minister of State across DBT + DESNZ). Each Office record carries a single `body_id` (the first org processed — PMO is forced first so the PM anchors to its literal home). The dual/joint hosting is not yet modelled; capture it later with multi-body links or Relationship edges, like the jointly-sponsored bodies. All current holders are recorded correctly as PersonRole records.
- **Ministers (task 4): office_type `other` tail (15).** Ceremonial/special posts (Lord President, Lord Privy Seal), the PM's secondary titles, law-adjacent and envoy roles the heuristic can't confidently tier. Flagged in each office's notes; refine against a cabinet-attendance source if a firmer tiering is wanted.
- **Sponsor refinement R1b (2026-07-06): CO directory cross-check.** `refine_sponsors.py` used the CO Public Bodies Directory parent-department field to check task-3 sponsor links: **198 confirmed** by a second official source, **2 filled** (Committee on Fuel Poverty, Committee on Radioactive Waste Management → DESNZ, previously roots). **6 discrepancies logged, not overridden** (GOV.UK live kept over the 2023/24 directory): Construction Industry Training Board & Engineering Construction ITB (GOV.UK DWP / CO DfE); Fair Work Agency (DBT / Home Office); Forest Research (Forestry Commission / Defra — different levels of the chain); Insolvency Rules Committee (MoJ / DBT); NISTA (Cabinet Office / HMT — jointly sponsored). Worth a human glance at Spiral 1 sign-off; most are currency or multi-level-parent artefacts, not errors. Unresolved CO department name: "Office of the Secretary of State for Scotland" (our Scotland Office) — name variant, 0 bodies affected. **RESOLVED by sponsor 2026-07-06:** keep both Industry Training Boards as DWP (GOV.UK, current); keep Insolvency Rules Committee as MoJ; NISTA stays joint Cabinet Office + HM Treasury (both edges already recorded from GOV.UK; entity panel now shows all department sponsors). Fair Work Agency (GOV.UK/DBT) and Forest Research (immediate-parent Forestry Commission) needed no change.
- **OPEN / UNSCHEDULED — historical & succession layer (parked 2026-07-06, sponsor).** The remaining closed bodies fall in two groups the live-state map deliberately omits but which matter later: (a) ~343 genuinely defunct/dissolved or time-limited bodies (Audit Commission, Assets Recovery Agency, public inquiries, reviews, Education Action Zones); (b) `merged`/`split` absorptions where a body's *functions and statutory powers transferred* to a successor (National School of Government → Cabinet Office; bodies split into several). Both matter for Spiral 2's "who inherited this power?" question and for retrodiction, but adding them as live nodes now would misrepresent the current state and clog the graph. **Parked as an open issue — no spiral assigned.** When resolved, likely a separate historical layer with `status: closed` nodes and succession relationships (needs a `succeeds`/`superseded_by` relationship_type, not yet in vocab), each sourced.

- **Department budget (OSCAR) and staffing (Civil Service Statistics) reachability.** To test from the sandbox at build start; if bulk files are not reachable, becomes a one-off manual download by the sponsor.
- **Classification-refinement pass — NEXT after the structural graph (decision A, 2026-07-06).** Task 2 classifies from the GOV.UK Organisations API `format` field alone (source-hierarchy rank 1 for identity, but *coarse* for governance class). 338 of 663 bodies carry `needs_classification_review=true` — the `other_body` catch-all (208) plus the `division_directorate`/`Sub organisation` tail (130). To get the governance-based split (like machineryofgovernment.uk, but rebuilt from our own sources — MoG is comparison/gap-finding only per A2.1, never copied), a follow-up pass should bring in the **Cabinet Office Public Bodies dataset (rank 2, official)**, which classifies ALBs (executive/advisory NDPB, tribunal, etc.) directly. Expected effect: shrink the flagged pile, and reconcile toward MoG's aggregation (e.g. the API lists 29 tribunal *chambers* where MoG aggregates to ~7 — a granularity choice we make with our own audit trail). Workpack task 7 (side-by-side vs MoG counts) feeds this.
- **`jurisdiction` not in the Organisations API.** Every task-2 Body record defaults `jurisdiction: ["UK"]` because the API carries no jurisdiction field. Per-body geographic scope (England-only, Scotland-only, etc.) is a later refinement — from body names, framework docs or legislation — not sourced in Spiral 1.
- **In-formation bodies included (11).** `govuk_status` joining/transitioning → Body `status: forming` (raw value in notes; rendered dashed). These are bodies mid-formation (e.g. Local Audit Office, National Shipbuilding Office); revisit their classification when they go live.
- **Relationships (task 3) — edges to closed bodies dropped (180).** The sponsor/parent graph keeps only edges where both endpoints are held (live/forming) Body records. 180 parent/child links point at `closed` orgs (Crown Commercial Service, Infrastructure and Projects Authority, Government Equalities Office, etc. — all superseded by MoG changes) and are dropped as historical links (workpack §2). If a full historical graph is later wanted, these can be recovered from the raw cache.
- **Jointly-sponsored bodies (14).** 7 bodies have >1 department parent (e.g. Office for Zero Emission Vehicles → DESNZ + DfT; Public Sector Fraud Authority → Cabinet Office + HMT) and 7 have >1 non-department parent. **All** parent edges are kept as Relationship records; the Body's single `sponsor_department_id`/`parent_body_id` convenience field takes the first alphabetically (deterministic) — the entity page should read the Relationship records to show every sponsor, not just the body field.
- **6 live bodies whose only parent is closed** may show as roots (no live sponsor edge). Minor; revisit in the refinement pass.
